ABOUT US

our history

The history of Norway dates back to the Viking era, when Norwegian warriors embarked on
long sea voyages in search of riches and adventures. During the Middle Ages, Norway
became an independent kingdom that expanded through the conquest of territories in the
British Isles and along the Scandinavian coast.
In the 14th century, Norway entered into a personal union with Denmark, which led to a period
of decline in the Norwegian economy and culture. During the Nazi occupation in World War II,
Norway suffered enormous human and material losses.
After the war, Norway became a democratic and prosperous state with a high level of social
and economic welfare. In 1994, Norway became a member of the European Free Trade
Association (EFTA) and in 1995 joined the World Trade Organization (WTO). Currently,
Norway is a constitutional monarchy with a mixed economy and a strong presence in the oil
and maritime sectors.
